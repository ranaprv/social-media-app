# How to: Configure Brand Voice

Train the AI to write in your brand's specific style, tone, and vocabulary.

---

## Prerequisites

- A running instance with at least one LLM API key configured
- A workspace created

---

## Setting Up Brand Voice

1. Navigate to **Settings** in the sidebar
2. Click **Brand Voice** (or go to `/dashboard/settings`)
3. Configure each section:

### Tone & Style

- **Primary Tone**: Professional, Casual, Friendly, Authoritative, Playful
- **Secondary Tones**: Mix up to 2 additional tones for variety
- **Writing Style**: Short sentences, Long paragraphs, Conversational, Technical, Storytelling
- **Emoji Usage**: None, Minimal (1-2 per post), Moderate (3-5), Heavy (6+)

### Content Rules

- **CTA Style**: Direct ("Buy now"), Soft ("Check it out"), Question-based ("Ready to start?")
- **Hashtag Strategy**: None, Minimal (1-3), Moderate (5-10), Aggressive (10+)
- **Formatting**: Plain text, Bullet points, Numbered lists, Mixed

### Vocabulary

Add words and phrases your brand uses (and avoids):

- **Include**: "growth", "scale", "innovation", "team-first"
- **Avoid**: "synergy", "leverage", "disrupt", "game-changer"

### Example Posts

Provide 3-5 example posts that represent your ideal brand voice. The AI learns from these to match your style.

Paste examples for each platform:
- LinkedIn examples
- X/Twitter examples
- Instagram examples

---

## Saving and Applying

1. Click **Save Brand Voice**
2. The AI automatically applies your brand voice to all future content generation
3. Existing generated content is not retroactively changed

---

## Per-Platform Overrides

You can set different brand voices per platform:

1. In Brand Voice settings, click the platform tab (LinkedIn, X, etc.)
2. Override specific settings for that platform
3. Save — the platform-specific voice takes precedence

Common pattern: Professional on LinkedIn, Casual on X, Visual-first on Instagram.

---

## Verifying Brand Voice

After configuring, test it:

1. Go to **Content Studio**
2. Generate a post on your primary platform
3. Check that the tone, vocabulary, and formatting match your brand voice
4. If not specific enough, add more example posts or tighten vocabulary rules

---

## Troubleshooting

**AI ignores brand voice:**
- Ensure you saved the brand voice settings
- Add more example posts — 3+ examples per platform work best
- Check that vocabulary "avoid" list is populated — negative examples help more than positive ones

**Content still sounds generic:**
- Be more specific with example posts — use real posts you've published
- Add platform-specific overrides — the AI adapts differently per platform
